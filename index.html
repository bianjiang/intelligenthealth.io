Overview

Intelligent systems and their integration with health software, information technology, and mobile health technology have the potential to transform the way we use data to improve health outcomes. The aim of this special track on Intelligent systems and mHealth for Health Outcomes is to bring together experts in machine learning, artificial intelligence, intelligent systems, sensors and devices, but also clinical researchers and other biomedical experts with an interest in technological advancements to improve clinical and health outcomes. This special track is hosted at the 30th International Conference on Industrial, Engineering and Other Applications of Applied Intelligent systems at the University of Artois, France.

Authors are invited to submit their papers in English of up to 10 single spaced pages, of unpublished work. Work that was previously presented as an abstract / poster and that is extended to a full-size paper is acceptable however. Work in progress or presentation of possible research directions and exploration may be submitted as a short presentation (up to 6 pages).

We are working to create a special issue in one of the relevant high impact journals.




Scope

We invite submissions of the latest research pertaining to intelligent systems as they apply to healthcare and mHealth in the context of improving health outcomes.

Topics of interest to this special track include, but are not limited to:

  • Intelligent Systems in Healthcare
    • Mining electronic health record (EHR) and administrative claims data
    • Analyzing large biomedical databases
    • Record linkage / Entity Resolution
    • Intelligent systems for patient-physician communication
    • Data science for informed and shared decision-making
    • Intelligent systems to facilitate point-of-care interventions
    • Data fusion of heterogeneous health data
    • Remote intelligent medical diagnosis system
    • Decision support system
    • Security, privacy and trust for intelligent healthcare systems
  • Intelligent Systems in mHealth
    • Analyzing mobile device data
    • Using mobile data to improve health outcomes
    • Wearable Computing
    • Decision support system for mHealth
    • Social networks and to improve health outcomes
    • Intelligent methods to analyze social networks data
    • Security, privacy and trust for mHealth

Submission

Authors are invited to submit their papers in English of up to 10 single spaced pages, of unpublished work. Work that was previously presented as an abstract / poster and that is extended to a full-size paper is acceptable however. Work in progress or presentation of possible research directions and exploration may be submitted as a short presentation (up to 6 pages). All paper submissions must be submitted electronically through the easychair website, as indicated on the conference website. All submissions will all be peer-reviewed and final copies of papers for inclusion in the conference proceedings will be published by Springer-Verlag (formatting instructions are available at http://www.springer.com/computer/lncs?SGWID=0-164-6-793341-0) in their Lecture Notes in Artificial Intelligence series.
